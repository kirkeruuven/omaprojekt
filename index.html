What is Material Design?

Material Design is a design system created by Google to help designers and developers build consistent, beautiful, and user-friendly interfaces for websites and apps.

Design Principles - Google Material Design

1. Inspired by the Real World

Material Design mimics real-world objects like paper. Shadows and layers help create depth, making the interface feel familiar and easy to understand.

2. Bold, Graphic, and Intentional

Use strong colors, clear typography, and plenty of white space to make your designs clean and easy to navigate.

3. Motion Provides Meaning

Animations and transitions guide users through the interface. For example, when you click a button, it ripples to confirm your action.

4. Adaptive Design for All Screens

Material Design is responsive and works seamlessly on various screen sizes and devices.

5. Consistent Components

Material Design provides standardized components like buttons, cards, and sliders for a unified experience across apps.
